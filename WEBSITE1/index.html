HIV Prevention: Protect Yourself and Others

Effective prevention methods can stop HIV transmission

Learn Prevention Methods

HIV Prevention Methods

Safe Sex Practices

  • Correct condom use every time
  • Dental dams for oral sex
  • Use lubrication to prevent tears

PrEP (Pre-Exposure Prophylaxis)

  • Daily pill to prevent HIV
  • Over 99% effective when taken as prescribed
  • Available by prescription

PEP (Post-Exposure Prophylaxis)

  • Emergency treatment after potential exposure
  • Must be started within 72 hours
  • Taken for 28 days

Harm Reduction

  • Never share needles
  • Use needle exchange programs
  • Always use sterile equipment

Get Tested

Regular HIV testing is crucial for early detection and prevention. Many people with HIV don't show symptoms for years.

  • Frequency: At least once a year if sexually active
  • Types: Rapid tests, home tests, lab tests
  • Confidentiality: Testing is private

HIV Myths vs Facts

Myth: HIV can spread through casual contact

Fact: HIV is not spread through hugging, shaking hands, or sharing food.

Myth: Mosquitoes can transmit HIV

Fact: HIV cannot survive or replicate in mosquitoes.

Myth: HIV is a death sentence

Fact: With treatment, people with HIV can live long, healthy lives.

HIV Stages & Symptoms

Acute HIV Infection

  • Fever and chills
  • Rash (often on torso)
  • Sore throat
  • Muscle aches and joint pain
  • Swollen lymph nodes
  • Mouth ulcers

Chronic HIV Infection

  • Often asymptomatic for years
  • Swollen lymph nodes
  • Fatigue
  • Weight loss
  • Fever and night sweats
  • Diarrhea

AIDS (Advanced Stage)

  • Rapid weight loss
  • Recurring fever/night sweats
  • Extreme fatigue
  • Prolonged diarrhea
  • Memory loss and confusion
  • Opportunistic infections

Visual Symptoms

Skin Conditions

  • Red or brownish rash (often itchy)
  • Kaposi's sarcoma lesions
  • Dry, flaky skin
  • Shingles (painful blisters)
  • Folliculitis (inflamed hair follicles)

Oral Symptoms

  • Oral thrush (white patches)
  • Hairy leukoplakia (white tongue)
  • Canker sores
  • Gum disease
  • Dry mouth

Other Visible Signs

  • Rapid weight loss
  • Swollen lymph nodes
  • Yellowing of skin/eyes (jaundice)
  • Purple skin lesions (Kaposi's sarcoma)
  • Nail changes (discoloration)

Testing & Diagnosis

When to Get Tested

Get tested if you experience acute symptoms or have risk factors (unprotected sex, shared needles). Many people have no symptoms for years.

Test Types

Antibody tests, antigen/antibody tests, and nucleic acid tests (NAT). Most tests can detect HIV within 2-6 weeks of exposure.

Confirming Diagnosis

Positive results require follow-up testing. Viral load and CD4 count tests help determine disease progression.

At-Home Testing

Oral fluid and fingerstick blood tests available. Positive results need confirmation from a healthcare provider.
