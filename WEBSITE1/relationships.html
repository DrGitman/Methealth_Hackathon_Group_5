Healthy Relationships with HIV

Building and maintaining fulfilling relationships while living with HIV

Learn About Disclosure

HIV Disclosure

When to Disclose

  • Before sexual activity
  • When considering a serious relationship
  • When sharing needles or other equipment
  • When required by law (varies by location)

How to Disclose

  • Choose a private, comfortable setting
  • Be prepared with accurate information
  • Share your treatment status (e.g., undetectable viral load)
  • Allow time for questions and processing

Legal Considerations

  • Know your local disclosure laws
  • Understand privacy protections
  • Consider consulting a legal professional
  • Document important conversations if needed

Communication Strategies

With Partners

Open, honest communication about status, prevention methods, and emotional needs is key to healthy relationships.

With Family

Decide what to share based on your comfort level and need for support. Not everyone needs to know.

With Friends

Choose trusted friends who can provide emotional support without judgment.

With Healthcare Providers

Be completely open about your status and concerns to get the best care.

Support Systems

Partner Support

Building a supportive partnership through mutual understanding, shared responsibility for prevention, and emotional support.

Family Support

Educating family members about HIV can help them provide meaningful support while respecting your boundaries.

Community Support

Connecting with others living with HIV through support groups can reduce isolation and provide valuable insights.
