Thriving with HIV: Modern Treatment and Care

With proper treatment, people with HIV can live long, healthy lives

Learn About Treatment

HIV Treatment/Management

ART (Antiretroviral Therapy)

  • Combination of medications that suppress the virus
  • Must be taken daily as prescribed
  • Can make viral load undetectable

Medical Monitoring

  • Regular viral load tests
  • CD4 count monitoring
  • Other health screenings (liver, kidney, etc.)

Avoid Alcohol

  • Alcohol weakens the immune system
  • Excessive consumption can lead to liver damage
  • Interference with ART,leading to increased viral load,etc

Healthy Living with HIV

Nutrition

Eat a balanced diet with plenty of fruits, vegetables, and lean proteins to support your immune system.

Exercise

Regular physical activity helps maintain strength, reduce stress, and improve overall health.

Mental Health

Seek support for depression or anxiety. Support groups and counseling can help.

Sleep

Aim for 7-9 hours of quality sleep each night to help your body recover and stay healthy.

Relationships & Prevention

U=U (Undetectable = Untransmittable)

When a person with HIV takes ART as prescribed and maintains an undetectable viral load, they cannot sexually transmit HIV to partners.

Disclosure Considerations

  • Legal requirements vary by location
  • Consider safety and support systems
  • Healthcare providers can offer guidance

Family Planning

With proper medical care, people with HIV can have healthy HIV-negative children through assisted reproduction techniques.
