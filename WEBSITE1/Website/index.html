HIV Prevention: Protect Yourself and Others

Effective prevention methods can stop HIV transmission

Learn Prevention Methods

HIV Prevention Methods

Safe Sex Practices

  • Correct condom use every time
  • Dental dams for oral sex
  • Use lubrication to prevent tears

PrEP (Pre-Exposure Prophylaxis)

  • Daily pill to prevent HIV
  • Over 99% effective when taken as prescribed
  • Available by prescription

PEP (Post-Exposure Prophylaxis)

  • Emergency treatment after potential exposure
  • Must be started within 72 hours
  • Taken for 28 days

Sharing Of Food

  • Do not bite the same fruit.
  • Do not drink from the same bottle.
  • Cut the fruit into pieces/Pour the drink into a different container.

Harm Reduction

  • Never share needles
  • Use needle exchange programs
  • Always use sterile equipment

Get Tested

Regular HIV testing is crucial for early detection and prevention. Many people with HIV don't show symptoms for years.

  • Frequency: At least once a year if sexually active
  • Types: Rapid tests, home tests, lab tests
  • Confidentiality: Testing is private

HIV Myths vs Facts

Myth: HIV can spread through casual contact

Fact: HIV is not spread through hugging, shaking hands, or sharing food.

Myth: Mosquitoes can transmit HIV

Fact: HIV cannot survive or replicate in mosquitoes.

Myth: HIV is a death sentence

Fact: With treatment, people with HIV can live long, healthy lives.
