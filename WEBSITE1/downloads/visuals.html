HIV Inclusive

1. What is HIV?

Human Immunodeficiency Virus (HIV): Attacks the immune system, specifically CD4 cells (T-cells), weakening the body's ability to fight infections.

AIDS (Acquired Immunodeficiency Syndrome): The final stage of untreated HIV, marked by severe immune damage and opportunistic infections.

2. Transmission

How it spreads:

  • Unprotected sex (vaginal, anal, oral)
  • Sharing needles/syringes
  • Mother-to-child (pregnancy, birth, breastfeeding)
  • Blood transfusions/organ transplants (rare in screened systems)

How it DOESN'T spread:

  • Casual contact (hugging, kissing, sharing food)
  • Mosquito bites

3. Symptoms

Early (Acute HIV):

Fever, fatigue, swollen lymph nodes, rash (2–4 weeks post-exposure).

Chronic HIV:

Asymptomatic for years, but the virus damages the immune system.

AIDS:

Rapid weight loss, recurring infections (e.g., TB, pneumonia), cancers (e.g., Kaposi's sarcoma).

4. Testing & Diagnosis

Tests:

  • Rapid antibody tests (finger prick/oral fluid)
  • PCR/RNA tests (detect virus early, 10–14 days post-exposure)
  • CD4 count/viral load tests (monitor progression)

Window period: 10–90 days (depends on test type).

5. Treatment (ART)

Antiretroviral Therapy (ART): Daily medications suppress HIV, allowing near-normal life expectancy.

  • Key drugs: Integrase inhibitors, protease inhibitors, NRTIs, NNRTIs
  • U=U: Undetectable viral load = Untransmittable (cannot spread HIV sexually)

PEP (Post-Exposure Prophylaxis):

Emergency meds within 72 hours of exposure.

PrEP (Pre-Exposure Prophylaxis):

Daily pill (e.g., Truvada) to prevent HIV in high-risk groups.

6. Prevention

  • Use condoms/dental dams
  • Never share needles
  • Regular testing (especially for high-risk groups)
  • Circumcision (reduces heterosexual male risk by ~60%)
  • Mother-to-child prevention: ART during pregnancy

7. Living with HIV

  • Adherence: Taking ART consistently is critical
  • Stigma: Fight misinformation; HIV is not a moral failing
  • Support: Join patient advocacy groups

8. Myths vs. Facts

Myth: HIV only affects gay men.
Fact: Anyone can contract HIV (all genders, sexualities).
Myth: HIV is a death sentence.
Fact: With ART, people live long, healthy lives.
