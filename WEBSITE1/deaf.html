HIV/AIDS Resources for the Deaf Community

Important Notice for Deaf Users

This resource is designed specifically for deaf individuals. All videos included have captions and/or ASL interpretation. Text information is presented visually with clear organization.

ASL HIV Terminology

HIV: Fingerspell H-I-V

AIDS: Fingerspell A-I-D-S

Medicine: Flat hand taps opposite wrist (like taking pulse)

Prevention: Both hands in "5" shapes, palms out, push forward

Doctor: "D" hand taps wrist (like taking pulse)

HIV Prevention (ASL Video Below)

Key Prevention Methods:

  • Condoms: Use latex or polyurethane condoms every time
  • PrEP: Daily pill that prevents HIV (99% effective)
  • PEP: Emergency medicine after possible exposure (must start within 72 hours)
  • Clean needles: Never share needles or injection equipment
  • Testing: Regular HIV/STD testing with your partner(s)

Mental Health Support

Deaf individuals with HIV may face unique challenges. Seek support from:

  • Deaf-friendly counselors
  • Online deaf HIV support groups
  • Peer mentors who are also deaf and HIV+

How to Communicate with Doctors

Your Rights:

  • You have the right to a qualified ASL interpreter at medical visits (free of charge)
  • Clinics must provide accessible communication under ADA law
  • You can request written information or captioned videos

Tips:

  1. Request interpreter when making appointment
  2. Bring a notepad or use text app for quick questions
  3. Ask for visual explanations (diagrams, models)
  4. Request all instructions in writing
